The Design of Everyday Things
Chapter 2 — The Psychology of Everyday Actions

How do people do things? It is surprisingly difficult to answer this question because people do not think about it. The action happens naturally, without thought. When someone turns a doorknob to open a door, they do not consciously think about the action — they simply do it.

We bridge the gulf of execution through action and the gulf of evaluation through perception and interpretation. The gulf of execution is the difficulty of acting upon the world, and the gulf of evaluation is the difficulty of interpreting the state of the world.

Affordances determine what actions are possible. Signifiers communicate where the action should take place. This is not a subtle difference. The door that must be pulled has a handle — the handle affords pulling and signifies where to grip.

Good design bridges both gulfs simultaneously. The challenge for designers is that they know too much. They understand the system intimately and cannot see it through the eyes of someone approaching it for the first time.
